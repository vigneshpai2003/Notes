Lagrangian Mechanics

Let $M$ be the configuration space of a system, and let $L$ be its Lagrangian then the action $S$ is defined in a time interval as: \begin{align} S(x) := \int_{t_1}^{t_2} L(x, \dot{x}, t) \, dt \end{align} where $x: [t_1, t_2] \to M$ represents the time evolution of the configuration of the system.

$M$ is often restrained by holonomic constraints, these are constraints of the form $f(x, t) = 0$.

Principle of Stationary Action

If the particle's is perturbed by a small amount $\epsilon \beta(t)$: \begin{align} \delta S &:= \dv{S(x_0 + \epsilon \beta)}{\epsilon} \Big|_{\epsilon=0} \\ &= \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \pdv{L}{x} \beta + \pdv{L}{\dot{x}} \dot{\beta} \, dt \\ &= \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \left(\pdv{L}{x} - \dv{}{t}\pdv{L}{\dot{x}}\right) \beta \, dt \\ \end{align}
